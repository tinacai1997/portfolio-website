Hi there! I'm Tina.

I love taking on tough challenges and working with others so they don't have to üòä

This belief is a huge part of why I thought I wanted to be an engineer. I thought that if I took the time to study the mathematics, standards, and formulas behind engineering that others couldn't, I could work on projects that truly impacted others.

As an electrical building engineer, I worked with diverse teams of clients and customers for a variety of building spaces. My favorite part about taking on a new project was being presented with a unique problem and unique groups of people to solve those problems for. However, I felt a disconnect between my work and it's end-users, I wasn't creating the impact that I was looking for.

When COVID hit I began exploring other fields and discovered UX design. UX design felt like everything I loved about being an engineer and more. It took the collaborative and problem-solving aspects of my work added empathy and creativity. It had me solving problems in the perspective of others and creating beautiful and impactful experiences. It excited me.

I've since taken every day as an opportunity to learn about UX design. I practiced design-thinking, learned design tools, studied UX methodologies, and completed several case studies, as showcased here in my portfolio (hand-coded by yours truly ü§ó). My path toward design is a bit untraditional, but I believe the most important part in making life changes like this is to remain open-minded, willing to learn, and unafraid to make mistakes along the way. All the skills and experiences I've gained until now were necessary in helping me do so.

When I'm taking a break from designing, I like to cook new recipes (check out my Feed !), play video games with friends, and cuddle my kitty! üò∏
