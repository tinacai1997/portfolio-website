Mesh

Your early tech event networking hub

Problem

Early tech talent are not getting traction through traditional job application methods. The roles early PM, designer, and developers apply for often have 100+ applications. Eventually, most receive a templated rejection email.

This problem is close to our hearts as everyone in our team has persevered through this challenge. We created Mesh to help tech talent explore a more effective approach to launch their careers.

Research

We interviewed six potential users under the assumptions that they were currently job searching, didn't like writing resumes, and needed motivation to continue job searching.

Our research showed that participants actually didn't need new solutions for our assumptions, they needed help with networking. Participants that took the extra effort in reaching out to recruiters, hiring managers, and existing employees were more successful in landing interviews.

Design

After narrowing our scope, I started on user flows and wireframes for our MVP. However, after a change in our dev team, our original MVP was no longer feasible. The team discussed our options and agreed to pivot from a native iOS app to a responsive web app.

Meanwhile, I had already started on mobile hi-fi wireframes. I proposed that we continued to use our existing designs and delay larger screens designs to meet time-constraints.

Iteration

We started with a preference test to assess our visual design. Then, we conducted usability tests to understand whether users could navigate our prototype given scenarios and success criteria. We worked together to synthesize this data as I improved our design in a continuous cycle of iteration.

MVP

What makes Mesh valuable is its focus on creating professional relationships. Our next steps are to add features that track event contacts and encourage users to stay connected with them.

Our current MVP barely scratches the surface on helping our users network, but we've listened to what they've had to say so far and are eager to continue improving Mesh for them.

Lessons

  1. The dev and I had a few mix ups due to confusion from both sides. I know now the importance of making sure my designs are understood while also understanding dev capabilities.
  2. I learned how to efficiently design pivot due to unforeseen circumstances. This was largely due to the friendly and professional environment the team and I had created.
  3. Learning Figma component and library functions were key for keeping our brand consistent and saving design time.
