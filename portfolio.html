Design Portfolio

Creating a home for project work

the problem

How can I display my design skills, style, and aptitude to other designers and potential recruiters?

The goal of this online design portfolio is to provide a platform to showcase my work and introduce myself into the design world.
This project also serves as a medium for me to practice and demonstrate my understanding of front-end development and web design.

Developing
the style üíÉ

initial css

Sometimes, it's ok to stray away.

I began writing CSS rules to emulate the sample wireframes style and element positioning. But the more I wrote, refreshed my site, and saw my website coming together, the more I felt like it wasn't my style.
Although I appreciated the sample wireframes being lo-fi to allow room for creativity, I felt like I was making design decisions as I went and was constantly second guessing minor details.

low fidelity wireframes

Unsatisfied with the sample wireframes, I began sketching my own lo-fi wireframes in pencil and paper.
As I sketched my lo-fi, I annotated my micro-animation ideas to make my portfolio more unique and personal.

high fidelity wireframes

I knew I needed more refined wireframes to guide my portfolio's design. After all, I wouldn't pass a lo-fi wireframe straight to development, as that would lead to repeat work when the design transitions to hi-fi.
I used Adobe XD to bring what I sketched to life through hi-fi wireframes. With XD, I was able to depict exactly what I wanted my portfolio to look like, which provided a guideline for me to stick to, and ease of mind from not having to design as I go.

The structure
(again) üß±

mobile-first

I focused on coding my site with a mobile-first approach. My aim was to both keep my focus on content rather than visual flair (button hovers, animations, etc.) and ensure accessibility for the smallest available screen (iPhone 5SE).

basic html

Armed with an understanding of the new structure of my website, I modified the HTML using Atom to match. These modifications weren't too crazy, as I had decided to keep most of the actual structure as similar as I could.
