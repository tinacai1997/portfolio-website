WSDOT Wave 2 Go

Redesigning the ferry ticketing experience

Overview

The Washington State Department of Transportation (WSDOT) receives too many calls and emails requesting support for purchasing ferry tickets. Their current ticket site is difficult to navigate, therefore users cannot confidently follow through with purchasing ferry tickets without help.
The business goal for WSDOT is to increase self service success for their ferry ticketing site.
Role: UX Reseacher/Designer
Tools: Pencil and Paper, Miro
Timeframe: 3 Hours (Feb 2021)

Focus user group

Who are we designing for?
With just a few hours to complete this design challenge, I needed to pinpoint a specific user group to focus my design toward. This gave me a direction for ideation by narrowing the design scope, otherwise I could have redesigned for hours. I chose to split my user options by age group:
  • Young student
  • Middle aged professional
  • Elderly and retired
I chose to focus on elderly users because I felt they would struggle most with using a website and were more likely to contact WSDOT for inquiries. I anticipated the solutions I designed for this group would encapsulate problems younger users might face with the site as well.

Problem space

What are some struggles elderly users might face with buying ferry tickets online?
Here, I identified possible painpoints that might have led to the business goal. Elderly users:
  • Don't often plan their own trips
  • Are not well-versed in purchasing tickets online
  • Are unfamiliar with logistics that go along with boarding a ferry
The common theme here is that the elderly are missing crucial information in the steps required for buying and using ferry tickets.

Proto-Persona

After narrowing down the problem space, I very quickly came up with a proto-persona and scenario to figure out how to help our persona achieve his goals and as a result, identify areas to redesign the site and meet our business goal.

Storyboarding

In this section, I created a storyboard to illustrate the pain points for Alfred's specific scenario and further put myself in his shoes.

Problem & Hypothesis

Problem: Alfred needs an informative booking process when purchasing his online ferry tickets because he wants a clear understanding of his trip beforehand.
We will know this to be true when we see Alfred successfully purchase ferry tickets online without the help of others.
I believe that by creating a simple booking process that keeps Alfred up to speed with the information needed for purchasing and using online ferry tickets, we will achieve a ferry ticketing website that will allow Alfred to purchase his online tickets without relying on help from others.

Action Priority Matrix

After performing an existing site audit, I list some ideas on improving the existing site then graph them based on their impact vs effort.
A constraint that I set for myself when choosing which ideas to move forward with was to meet our business goals while not straying too far from the company's brand and existing userbase. So I moved forward with design ideas that improved on existing content rather than implement brand new features to stay focused on solving our defined problem statement.

Trade-Offs

  1. Existing Users: Existing users might find new additional steps to be annoying

    • I believe providing relevant information would primarily be beneficial to all user groups , but I can understand this could be an issue
    • Perhaps we could design an option where existing users could save their typical purchase

Challenges

  1. "Clarity": Looking back at my work in the design challenge, I think I did a good job of specifying my problem space. However, I think it was important that I better articulated my definition of "clarity" and where we needed to emphasize it in my design. I feel that some of the solutions and conclusions I made appeared really obvious through the way I worded it and I want my design work to be something that my clients value and find unique.

Next steps

  1. Other Ideas: If I had more time to expand on this project, I'd look toward some of the design ideas I didn't prioritize in this case study.

  2. Hi-fi wireframes: The next steps for this project would be to work on bringing our lo-fi wireframes into hi-fi. Since this site already exists, and my designs were intentionally kept minimal and specific, I believe we'd be ok to skip the mid-fi step and move onto hi-fi mockups, to provide visuals for our clients so they can have a clearer idea of the solution and move forward with testing.
