Zhen's Appliance Service

Your local repairman information re-designed

Problem

Zhen is the sole proprieter of Zhen's Appliance Service and manages appliance repair orders on a daily basis. Lately, he has been receiving too many customer inquiries for orders he cannot service.

How do we balance providing customers what they're looking for while also optimizing the client's time?

ROLE
UX Researcher
UX/UI Designer

TEAM
Jin Cai
Zhen (Client)

TIMELINE
February 2022
December 2022

Business Goals

I interviewed Zhen to understand his target customers and pain points regarding the overarching problem. My goal was to gauge Zhen's business goals so I could later connect them to those of his potential customers and provide a cohesive solution.

Research

I conducted a survey to uncover what customers value most when searching for in-home services. Appliance repair was quite specific, so I made the survey available to anyone who had received an in-home service of any kind.

I compiled the survey data using affinity maps and uncovered several major insights.

Insights

Customers feel a sense of urgency when in-home services are needed. They usually don't plan ahead of time, and when searching for services, look to whoever has the highest ratings and can help the quickest.

Survey participants had a lot more to add when they had an exceptional in-home service in mind. Being a present and friendly service person was especially memorable.

Design

Zhen doesn't have a product to sell his services other than Google and Yelp and wasn't particular about the design solution. So, I jumped straight into hifi wireframing to better present my ideas.

We settled on creating a responsive website, as it was the most feasible for our team to complete and accessible on multiple customer devices. I focused on a simple site architecture that displays all important customer values upfront.

Iteration

Improving the new site design through usability testing.

I conducted several usability tests to ensure users were able to meet their goals when visiting the new site. The client had many requirements, but it's also important that those were met with the user in mind as well.

Solution

Delivering exactly what the client needs while increasing customer value and usability.

It was important that we kept our client's typical customer workflow unchanged while mitigating pain points. For the customer, we needed to quickly deliver the information they were looking for so they can get their appliances fixed fast.

We believe we succeeded in accumulating insights from both perspectives to create a solution that works for all.

Lessons

  1. As this was a volunteer project, it took much longer to finish than originally planned. I learned the importance of communication and setting deadlines in keeping the team accountable.
  2. This being my first client project, I gained many new insights on balancing both business and user goals.
