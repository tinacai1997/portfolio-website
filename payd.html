Payd

Your all inclusive e-wallet application

PROBLEM

Digital wallet users face an overabundance of finance apps in their mobile phones

Online sales and transactions have steadily increased over the last decade. The COVID pandemic had increased this rate even further, resulting in more and more apps designed to manage finances.
This led to the question: why do users need to handle so many different apps in order to understand their finances?
Role: UX/UI Designer (UX Bootcamp Solo Project)
Tools: Adobe XD, Balsamiq, Google Sheets
Timeframe: Jun 2021 - Oct 2021

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

The competition did not tell the whole financial story

I analyzed three popular finance management apps and found that though each app performs specific functions well, just one was not enough for users to understand their entire financial summary.

USER INTERVIEWS

Finance app users care about convenience and tracking for financial transactions

I conducted user interviews to understand what three finance app users valued most in their apps. My research goals were to gauge vital features, contexts of use, and pain points surrounding competitor apps.

KEY RESEARCH INSIGHTS

Users want basic finance app features on top of a comprehensive financial overview

I noticed from my research that users used finance apps on an as-needed basis and preferred different apps for accomplishing specific tasks. Users also expressed wanting to improve their spending habits, but had no way of viewing the bigger picture.

PERSONAS + JOURNEY MAPS

Synthesizing research data into personable goals and scenarios

I personified my research data and help me narrow who I was designing for. I also shaped journey maps to illustrate relatable scenarios for each persona. These journeys helped me brainstorm pain points and opportunities for varying use cases.

IDEATION

Mapping out primary user goals through three user flows

I identified three main functions to focus on based on our user research themes. I used the opportunities brainstormed during journey mapping to sketch user flows for these core functions.

DESIGN

Wireframing a comprehensive financial management mobile app

Though I was tasked with creating a web app, I felt a mobile solution made more sense for the solution. Mobile finance apps were more popular among interview participants and allowed for a mobile-first design approach. I created mid-fi wireframes, then started on hi-fi and prototyping when I felt the functionality was moving toward the right direction.

TESTING + ITERATION

Making three major improvements to the design

I observed how six finance app users navigated through the prototype during usability testing. I organized trends using affinity maps and a rainbow spreadsheet to prioritize usability errors. I continuously iterated based on this feedback and visual and accessibility guidelines to address these errors.

learnings

    I gained a better understanding of what processes to use, when to use them, and how they deliver insights that affect the big picture.
