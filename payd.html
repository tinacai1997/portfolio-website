Payd

Your all inclusive e-wallet application

Problem

Digital wallet users face an overabundance of finance apps in their mobile phones.

Online sales and transactions have steadily increased over the last decade. The COVID pandemic had increased this rate even further, resulting in more and more apps designed to manage finances.

This led to the question: why do users need to handle so many different apps in order to understand their finances?

Analysis

The competition did not tell the whole financial story.

I analyzed three popular finance management apps and found that though each app performs specific functions well, just one was not enough for users to understand their entire financial summary.



User Interviews

Finance app users care about convenience and financial tracking.

I conducted user interviews to understand what three finance app users valued most in their apps. My research goals were to gauge vital features, contexts of use, and pain points surrounding competitor apps.

Synthesis

Synthesizing research data into personable goals and scenarios using personas and journey maps.

I personified my research data and help me narrow who I was designing for. I also shaped journey maps to illustrate relatable scenarios for each persona. These journeys helped me brainstorm pain points and opportunities for varying use cases.

Insights

Design

Mapping out primary user goals through three user flows.

I personified my research data and help me narrow who I was designing for. I also shaped journey maps to illustrate relatable scenarios for each persona. These journeys helped me brainstorm pain points and opportunities for varying use cases.

Iteration

Making three major improvements to the design based on usability testing.

I observed how six finance app users navigated through the prototype during usability testing. I organized trends using affinity maps and a rainbow spreadsheet to prioritize usability errors. I continuously iterated based on this feedback and visual and accessibility guidelines to address these errors.

Solution

A mobile financial app that rolls up other finance apps into one neat bundle.

Payd provides an overview of all expenses by connecting the user's payment methods and by being one itself

Lessons

  1. I learned the difference between research and interview questions. I now know the importance of keeping interview questions open ended to effectively gather qualitative information.
  2. I had a habit of tunnel visioning. I should have prioritized functionality and feedback-based changes over minor visual details to keep from being overwhelmed during iteration.
  3. I gained a better understanding of what processes to use, when to use them, and how they deliver insights that affect the big picture.
